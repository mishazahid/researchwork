Primary User Traffic Pattern Based Opportunistic Spectrum Handoff in Cognitive Radio Networks

Through the expeditious expansion of the wireless network, the unlicensed bandwidth-based devices are growing substantially as compared to the present vacant bandwidth. Cognitive radio networks present a proficient solution to the spectrum shortage diminution hitch by allowing the usage of the vacant part of the spectrum that is not currently in use of the Primary User licensed bandwidth to the secondary user or cognitive radio user. Spectrum management procedure in cognitive radio network comprises of spectrum sharing, sensing and handoff. Spectrum handoff plays a vital role in spectrum management and primarily focuses on single handoff strategies. This paper presents a primary user traffic pattern-based opportunistic spectrum handoff (PUTPOSH) approach to use in the cognitive radio networks. PUTPOSH permits a secondary user to sense the arrival of a primary user and use an opportunistic handoff scheme. The opportunistic handoff scheme firstly detects the arrival of the primary users by energy detection sensing and secondly, it allows a cognitive radio user to decide whether to do handoff or not contingent upon the overall service time to reduce the unused handoffs. The handoffs can either be reactive or proactive based on the arrival rate of the primary user. The simulation results show that the presented PUTPOSH approach (a) minimizes the number of handoffs and the overall service time, and (b) maintains the channel utilization and throughput of the system at a maximal point.

Integration of Data Mining Clustering Approach in the Personalized E-Learning System

Educational data-mining is an evolving discipline that focuses on the improvement of self-learning and adaptive methods. It is used for finding hidden patterns or intrinsic structures of educational data. In the arena of education, the heterogeneous data is involving and continuously growing in the paradigm of big-data. To extract meaningful information adaptively from big educational data, some specific data mining techniques are needed. This paper presents a clustering approach to partition students into different groups or clusters based on their learning behavior. Furthermore, personalized e-learning system architecture is presented which detects and responds to teaching contents according to the students' learning capabilities. The primary objective includes the discovery of optimal settings, in which learners can improve their learning capabilities. Moreover, the administration can find essential hidden patterns to bring the effective reforms in the existing system. The clustering methods K-Means, K-Medoids, Density-based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise, Agglomerative Hierarchical Cluster Tree and Clustering by Fast Search and Finding of Density Peaks via Heat Diffusion (CFSFDP-HD) are analyzed using educational data mining. It has been observed that more robust results can be achieved by the replacement of existing methods with CFSFDP-HD. The data mining techniques are equally effective in analyzing the big data to make education systems vigorous.

How Artificial Intelligence would revolutionize nursing care in Pakistan

The nursing care in Pakistan is facing many challenges and has an immense impact on the health sector in general. Pakistan is not producing an adequate number of nurses to cater to the growing population. The purpose of this study is to explore the difficulties faced by the nursing staff regarding workload, efficiency and doctor unavailability and how artificial intelligence applications may be used to overcome these problems. This is a qualitative research in which a purposive sample was interviewed. The data obtained from the interviews was analyzed to reach a conclusion. This study found out that AI applications such as remote monitoring, predictive algorithms and expert systems could be implemented to solve the problems faced by the nurses. The execution of the proposed AI systems in this paper would be an enhancement to the workforce that would ultimately benefit healthcare as a whole.
Key words - artificial intelligence, nursing care, healthcare, remote monitoring, predictive algorithm.

UML Design report for an online shopping cart system

The report proposes the design for an online shopping cart system. The system presented facilitates the placement and payment of orders using an online shopping cart system. The system will allow users to view the product catalogue online and make purchases accordingly along with a payment gateway. It will enable users to shop through an online website at their ease. The system allows the user to navigate through different categories and allow the user to select their favourite items or the items they want to buy and add them to a shopping cart. The user will also need to create a user account in order to do shopping online where the payment details and the user details of the customer will be saved. Once the items have been selected in the login the user will checkout and choose between doing the payment online or by cash. The online cart system would be both cost and time effective. This system will help in organizing and effective record keeping. Providing a secure payment gateway will help both the customer and companies in secure and hassle free transactions. Moreover, because of this system more accurate financial reports will be produced as each and every transaction will be recorded eliminating the fear of misplacement and tempering of documents. At the customer’s end, both local and international clients will benefit. The system will make access to the product catalogues easier, along with simple placement and payment of orders
